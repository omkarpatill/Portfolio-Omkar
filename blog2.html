Dummy text is used in the publishing industry or by web designers to occupy space which will later be filled with real content. This is required when the final text is not yet available. It is also known as "fill text".

Dummy text helps compare typefaces and layouts without distraction. Since human perception notices patterns and repetitions in text, dummy text is often randomized to avoid this distraction.

The most well-known dummy text is "Lorem Ipsum", originating in the 16th century. While pseudo-Latin, it imitates the rhythm of European languages and remains readable enough for layout mockups, without distracting from design.
